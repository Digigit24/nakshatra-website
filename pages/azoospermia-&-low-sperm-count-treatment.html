Azoospermia & Low Sperm Count Treatment...

Many couples struggle with infertility, facing challenges in conceiving naturally. Infertility can be caused by issues in either the female or male partner, and studies show that male-related infertility contributes to nearly 50% of total cases. Among these, azoospermia the complete absence of sperm in semen and low sperm count are among the most common causes.
At Nakshatra Clinic, Pune, we provide expert low sperm count treatment in Pune, specialising in diagnosing and treating male infertility with advanced procedures such as micro TESE, TESA, PESA, and ICSI. Even with azoospermia or low sperm count, modern reproductive techniques can help men achieve fatherhood and fulfil their dream of having a biological child.

Advanced Treatment Options for Azoospermia & Low Sperm Count

Micro TESE (Microsurgical Testicular Sperm Extraction)

Micro TESE is a microsurgical sperm retrieval procedure in Pune that extracts sperm directly from the testicular tissue. It is highly effective for men with non-obstructive azoospermia and provides sperm for ICSI or IVF treatments, improving fertility outcomes.

Testicular Sperm Aspiration (TESA)

TESA is a minimally invasive procedure that extracts sperm from the testicles using a fine needle under local anesthesia. It is suitable for men with low sperm production and provides viable sperm for fertility treatments like ICSI or IVF.

Percutaneous Epididymal Sperm Aspiration (PESA)

PESA is a quick and safe procedure for obstructive azoospermia, where sperm is collected directly from the epididymis using a fine needle. The retrieved sperm can be used for ICSI to increase fertilization success.

ICSI & Lifestyle & Supportive Therapies

ICSI is an advanced assisted reproductive technique where a single sperm is injected directly into an egg to achieve fertilization. It is ideal for azoospermia or low sperm count and significantly improves pregnancy chances.

Frequently Asked Questions

Azoospermia is the absence of sperm in semen , caused by obstruction, hormonal issues, or testicular failure. Treatment involves Micro TESE, TESA, or PESA to retrieve sperm, followed by ICSI or IVF , combined with medical therapy and lifestyle optimization to increase conception chances.

Low sperm count can result from hormonal imbalances, varicocele, infections, medications, genetic issues, or lifestyle factors like smoking and stress. Proper diagnosis through semen analysis, hormonal tests, and ultrasound allows doctors to recommend effective treatments, including assisted reproductive techniques or corrective surgery.

Micro TESE (Microsurgical Testicular Sperm Extraction) is a precise surgery to collect sperm from testicular tissue in men with non-obstructive azoospermia . It maximizes sperm retrieval success and provides sperm for ICSI , enabling biological parenthood even with zero sperm in semen.

ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection) injects a single sperm into an egg to achieve fertilization. It is highly effective for men with low sperm count or azoospermia , improving fertilization and pregnancy rates significantly when combined with sperm retrieval procedures like TESA or PESA.

TESA and PESA are minimally invasive sperm retrieval techniques for obstructive and non-obstructive azoospermia. When combined with ICSI , the success rate for fertilization and pregnancy can reach 50–70% , depending on sperm quality and the female partner’s reproductive health.
