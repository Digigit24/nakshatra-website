Types of Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT)

1. PGT-A (Aneuploidy)

This test screens embryos for chromosomal abnormalities. Healthy embryos should contain 23 pairs of chromosomes — one from the egg and one from the sperm. Missing or extra chromosomes (aneuploidy) can lead to Down Syndrome, Turner Syndrome, or miscarriage. PGT-A helps transfer chromosomally normal embryos, improving IVF success rates.

2. PGT-M (Monogenic Disorders)

Targets specific inherited diseases when one or both partners are carriers or there’s a family history. Supports transferring unaffected embryos and breaking the chain of transmission.

3. PGT-SR (Structural Rearrangements)

PGT-M detects specific inherited genetic disorders that one or both partners may carry.

It is especially recommended if:

  • You have a family history of genetic conditions.
  • You are a carrier of an X-linked or autosomal recessive disease

This test ensures only unaffected embryos are transferred, helping prevent passing the disorder to the child.

Frequently Asked Questions

Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT) is an advanced embryo screening technique performed during an IVF cycle to identify genetic or chromosomal abnormalities before embryo transfer. It ensures that only healthy embryos are selected for implantation, improving IVF success rates and reducing the risk of miscarriage or inherited genetic disorders. This test is especially helpful for couples facing infertility or repeated IVF failures.

The main benefits of Preimplantation Genetic Testing include:

  • Higher IVF success and implantation rates
  • Reduced chances of miscarriage
  • Prevention of inherited genetic diseases
  • Fewer IVF cycles needed to achieve pregnancy
  • - Selection of the healthiest embryos for transfer PGT gives couples peace of mind and increases the likelihood of a successful, healthy pregnancy.

The success rate of PGT with IVF depends on factors like maternal age, embryo quality, and lab expertise. On average, couples undergoing PGT experience implantation rates of 65–80%, compared to 40–50% without testing. Clinics with experienced embryologists and genetic counselors, such as Nakshatra Clinic in Pune, consistently achieve higher pregnancy success rates.

Though highly accurate (up to 98%), PGT is not 100% foolproof. Some embryos may yield inconclusive results or have mosaic patterns (a mix of normal and abnormal cells). Additionally, a small chance of false positive or false negative exists. Consulting a genetic counselor before and after the test helps interpret results correctly and plan next steps confidently.

PGT is usually scheduled during an IVF or ICSI cycle, right after fertilization and before embryo transfer. Your fertility team will plan the exact timeline based on your cycle stage and hormonal profile. Early consultation ensures optimal timing and preparation for accurate results.
