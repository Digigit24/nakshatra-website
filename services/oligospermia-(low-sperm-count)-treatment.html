Oligospermia (Low Sperm Count) Treatment

re you struggling to conceive despite trying for months or years? You’re not alone. Many couples face fertility challenges, and in almost 50% of cases, male infertility is a contributing factor. One of the most common causes is oligospermia, which simply means a low sperm count.
At Nakshatra Clinic, we understand the emotional and physical challenges that come with infertility. Our expert male fertility specialists provide personalized oligospermia treatment in Pune, combining medical therapy, lifestyle guidance, surgical options, and assisted reproductive techniques to help you improve sperm health and achieve your dream of fatherhood.

Provide Oligospermia Treatment

Personalized Treatment Plan

Based on the evaluation, a customized treatment plan is created, combining medical therapy, surgical procedures, lifestyle guidance, and assisted reproductive techniques (ICSI/IVF) to maximize sperm count, motility, and fertility potential.

Medical Therapy

Hormonal imbalances or deficiencies are treated with targeted medications under expert supervision. This helps restore healthy sperm production and improves overall reproductive health naturally and safely.

Lifestyle & Nutritional Guidance

We provide practical advice on diet, exercise, stress management, and supplements like Coenzyme Q10, L-carnitine, zinc, and folic acid, which can improve sperm quality, motility, and overall fertility outcomes.

Surgical Interventions

When structural issues like varicocele affect sperm production, our doctor performs minimally invasive surgical procedures such as varicocelectomy to restore proper sperm flow and improve fertility.

Assisted Reproductive Techniques (ART)

For cases where natural conception is challenging, we provide ICSI or IVF . These advanced techniques allow sperm to fertilize eggs successfully, offering hope for couples struggling with oligospermia.

Ongoing Monitoring & Follow-Up

Regular follow-ups track improvements in sperm count and motility. Treatment plans are adjusted as needed, and patients receive continuous support and guidance throughout their fertility journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

Oligospermia is a condition where a man has a low sperm count in semen, usually fewer than 15 million sperm per milliliter . While it can reduce fertility, it is treatable through medical therapy, lifestyle improvement, surgical correction, and assisted reproductive techniques.

Low sperm count can result from hormonal imbalances, varicocele, infections, medications, genetic factors , or lifestyle habits like smoking, alcohol, poor diet, and chronic stress . Identifying the cause helps doctors create an effective and targeted treatment plan.

Diagnosis includes semen analysis, hormonal tests, scrotal ultrasound, and genetic screening . A full medical history and physical examination help determine the underlying cause of low sperm count so the most suitable treatment can be planned.
